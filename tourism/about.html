About Kerala

Discover the beauty, culture, and heritage of Kerala.

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is a state renowned for its extraordinary natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and remarkable social development. Fondly known as "God's Own Country", Kerala is blessed with lush green landscapes, tranquil backwaters, pristine beaches, mist-covered hill stations, and diverse wildlife.

The history of Kerala dates back thousands of years and reflects influences from various cultures and civilizations that traded through its ancient ports. This rich historical background has contributed to a vibrant cultural identity expressed through traditional art forms such as Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, Theyyam, and Kalaripayattu.

Kerala is also recognized for its achievements in education, healthcare, and human development. The state boasts one of the highest literacy rates in India and has earned global recognition for its quality healthcare services and progressive social indicators.

Agriculture plays an important role in Kerala's economy, with products such as coconut, rubber, tea, coffee, cardamom, and pepper contributing significantly to its growth. The state's cuisine, characterized by the use of fresh spices, coconut, and seafood, offers a delightful culinary experience.

Whether it is cruising through the backwaters of Alappuzha, exploring the tea plantations of Munnar, relaxing on the beaches of Kovalam, or witnessing the majestic Athirappilly Waterfalls, Kerala offers unforgettable experiences for every traveler.
